Heroin: A Scourge on Society

by Nicolas Aguirre

Photograph by Brad Dozier

Heroin is arguably the most destructive and problematic drug to the human race. While drug abuse runs rampant across the globe, few other drugs can compare with the magnitude of heroin. An addictive, deadly, and popular drug, it transcends barriers of region, age, race and class. Lives are lost and societies are ravaged as heroin use and production continues to grow.

Heroin (diacetylmorphine) is an opioid analgesic (painkiller) that can be smoked, snorted and injected. The drug attracts initial users with the appeal of overwhelming physical euphoria, and seizes addicts with the threat of horrific, deadly withdrawal symptoms. Users build tolerance to the drug and require greater quantities to maintain their habit. The demand for potent, and therefore deadly heroin is always growing. Fatal overdoses are shockingly common among addicts.

Who Uses Heroin?

Many envision addicts as impoverished transients from low-income neighborhoods. In reality, heroin use is growing in popularity among suburban, middle-class individuals in the U.S. The prominence of narcotic painkillers in America has spawned a new generation of addicts. Heroin use is not restricted to certain demographics. Surprisingly, recent data reveals that the percentage of white heroin users is greater than the percentage of white individuals in the overall U.S. population.
